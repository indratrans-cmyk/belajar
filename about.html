History

Decades of innovation.

1976

Apple is Founded

Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne found Apple Computer Company in a garage in Cupertino, California. The Apple I personal computer is sold as a kit.

1984

Macintosh Arrives

The original Macintosh is introduced with the legendary "1984" Super Bowl ad. It becomes the first mass-market personal computer to feature a graphical user interface.

2001

iPod & iTunes

Apple launches the iPod and iTunes, transforming the music industry forever. "1,000 songs in your pocket" becomes one of tech's most iconic phrases.

2007

iPhone Changes Everything

Steve Jobs unveils the original iPhone — a revolutionary multi-touch device that reimagines what a phone could be. The smartphone era begins.

2020

Apple Silicon

Apple transitions the Mac lineup to its own Apple Silicon chips starting with M1 — delivering breakthrough performance and efficiency that redefines personal computing.

Values

What we stand for.

Privacy

Privacy is a fundamental human right and one of our core values. We design our products and services so that you're in control of your personal information.

Accessibility

Technology is most powerful when it's available to everyone. We're committed to building products that work for people with a wide range of abilities.

Environment

We take full responsibility for our impact on the planet. Apple's corporate operations are carbon neutral, and we're working toward a fully carbon-neutral supply chain by 2030.

Education

We believe education is a great equalizer. Apple invests in tools and programs that help students and educators unlock their creative potential in the classroom and beyond.
